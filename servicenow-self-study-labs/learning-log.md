# Learning Log

## Week 1 – Platform Basics

### What I studied
- ServiceNow as a cloud-based enterprise workflow platform
- Difference between platform view, service portal, and employee center
- Basic navigation: application navigator, lists, forms, filters, favorites

### What I practiced
- Opened different modules from the Application Navigator
- Compared list view and form view
- Reviewed how records are displayed and updated
- Created notes about common ITSM tables and modules

### What I learned
ServiceNow is not just a ticketing tool. It is a platform for managing digital workflows across IT, HR, security, and enterprise operations.

## Week 2 – ITSM Basics

### What I studied
- Incident Management
- Change Management
- Request Management
- Service Catalog basics

### What I practiced
- Reviewed typical incident fields such as caller, category, priority, assignment group, and state
- Studied the lifecycle of an incident from creation to resolution
- Compared incidents with requests and changes

### What I learned
Incident Management focuses on restoring normal service as quickly as possible. Requests are usually standard user needs, while changes require planning and approval.

## Week 3 – Administration Basics

### What I studied
- Users
- Groups
- Roles
- Access Control Lists (ACL)

### What I practiced
- Documented the difference between users and groups
- Created examples of role-based access
- Studied why ACLs are important for enterprise security

### What I learned
Access management is a core part of ServiceNow administration. Roles and ACLs help control who can see, create, update, or delete data.

### What I studied

- Flow Designer
- Trigger, action, and condition concepts

### What I practiced
